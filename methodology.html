Methodology

How CPI works.

CPI is designed to estimate team strength using available tournament results while remaining transparent enough for parents and coaches to review.

What CPI measures

The California Polo Index combines prior team strength, game results, opponent quality, tournament context, and movement after major events. It is not an official governing-body ranking.

Current release

This release uses the post–Futures Super Finals 14U Boys dataset. The rankings compare each team against its pre-tournament CPI and update based on Super Finals results.

Why rankings can differ from tournament finish

Tournament placement matters, but CPI also considers who a team played, who they beat, and where teams were ranked before the event. A team can finish higher in one tournament while another team retains a higher CPI because of broader season performance or strength of schedule.
